## Piece 066 — Pendulum-blade tube/casing (anchor assembly)

### Role

Folded tube/casing inside the anchor assembly. Wraps around to form a closed cross-section; receives tabs from the zig-zag anchor arm (piece 65) at multiple points along its length, plus one from piece 68 (anchor fork). One closure tab wraps around to seal the tube.

### Book context

- **Embedded labels**: *"Long flat development of the pendulum-blade tube/casing. `---`: horizontal fold lines along the full length (creates the tube when rolled)."* Plate F.
- **Prose**: part of the `Assemble the anchor, pieces 65, 66, 67, 68, and 69` group.
- **Legacy authoring** captured these tab + landing labels: 8 tabs (`a/b/c/d/e/f/g/aa`); 7 cross-piece landings from 65 (`b/c/d/e/f/g/h`); 1 cross-piece landing from 68 (`j`); 1 same-piece closure (`landing-aa`). I'm using those as ground truth.

### Anticipated panels (~10)

**Body panels** (uncertain count — depends on cross-section):
- `body` — single long strip if the tube has a single longitudinal fold line forming a flattened cross-section
- *or* `face1`, `face2`, `face3`, `face4` — if it's a square-cross-section tube with 3 longitudinal fold lines defining the 4 faces

The horizontal-fold-lines note suggests the second is more likely. **My guess: 4 face panels**, but I won't be sure until I see how you draw the silhouette + folds. If you find a single `body` panel cleaner, take it.

**Tab panels** (high confidence — 8):
- `taba`, `tabb`, `tabc`, `tabd`, `tabe`, `tabf`, `tabg`, `tabaa`

`tabaa` is the closure tab (the long one that wraps around to seal the cylinder). The others are smaller glue tabs along the edge.

### Anticipated folds (~14, all valley unless noted)

**Body-internal folds** (longitudinal, define the tube cross-section):
- `fold-face1-face2`
- `fold-face2-face3`
- `fold-face3-face4`

(If body is a single `body` panel, these don't exist as authored folds.)

**Tab folds** (transverse, attach each tab to its body panel):
- `fold-<body-panel>-taba` ... through `tabg`
- `fold-<body-panel>-tabaa` (closure-tab fold)

You'll know which body panel each tab attaches to from the geometry. Each tab gets exactly one fold to one body panel.

### Anticipated `attach-points`

Cross-piece connections — landings receiving tabs FROM other pieces, plus the shared rotation pivot:

- `pivot-anchor` (shared with 65/67/68/69 — entire anchor assembly rotates as one unit on the rear axle; piece 65 carries the *"axe R"* label)

### Anticipated `marks`

- `landing-aa` (same-piece closure — `tabaa` wraps around and lands here, closing the tube)
- Possibly registration `align-X<piece>` markers if 066 needs to register against 67 or 69 visually — none in the legacy authoring, so probably not

### Open questions for the canvas

1. **Body panel count.** 1 or 4? Look at the silhouette + horizontal fold lines and pick whatever maps cleanly to physical regions. If you collapse to 1 body panel, the tube cross-section folds happen automatically when the slider rotates everything around the body axis — but the assembly engine sees fewer hinge axes to work with.
2. **Tab `aa` vs. `a`.** Why does the print use `aa` rather than `a` for the closure tab? Is `tab-a` already used elsewhere on this piece (i.e., is there a separate `tab-a` distinct from the wrap-around)? 3. **`landing-j68` placement.** Tab "j" doesn't appear in 068's cell labels (`f/a/b/c/d/e/g/h`). Either the legacy authoring named it inconsistently, or piece 68 has a tab `j` that didn't make it into the embedded-labels notes. **Confirm against the print.**
4. **Folds-mountain layer.** Legacy 066 had only valley folds (15 in folds-valley, 0 ided in folds-mountain). For panels-first, all folds in a single direction stay in one layer. **Confirm no mountain folds on this piece.**

## Piece 068 — Anchor fork (anchor assembly)

### Role

Fork-shaped folded structure at the front of the anchor assembly. Passes over frame beam piece 9; receives the pendulum rod (piece 70) between its two prongs; pivots with the rest of the anchor on the rear axle.

### Book context

- **Embedded labels**: *"Anchor fork (rectangular fold-up development). Labels: f, a, b, c, d, e, g, h on the cells. `---`: on all flanges."* Plate H.
- **Prose §II.C**: *"The fork, piece 68, passes over the beam, piece 9, of the frame and will swing between the wall and the frame. ... The pendulum rod passes through the end of the fork, piece 68. It must fit between the prongs of the fork without binding."*
- 8 cell labels — `a/b/c/d/e/f/g/h` — strongly suggest 8 distinct panels.

### Anticipated panels (~9)

**Body panels** (8 — one per cell label):
- `a`, `b`, `c`, `d`, `e`, `f`, `g`, `h`

The eight cell-letter panels probably map to: the box-shaped body of the fork (4-5 panels for top/bottom/sides) + the two prongs (2-3 panels for the prong faces). Without the visual I can't tell you which letter is which physical face, but the 1:1 mapping of letter→panel is the cleanest authoring convention.

**Glue tabs** (uncertain — likely 1-3):
- Closure tabs along the perimeter where the box closes up; named `tab-<letter>` if they have explicit print labels, or skipped if the body panels meet edge-to-edge without separate glue tabs.

### Anticipated folds (~7-10, all valley)

Folds binding adjacent body cells into the 3D fork shape. Without seeing the development I can't enumerate exactly, but the count is approximately one fold per shared edge between adjacent letter-cells:

- `fold-a-b`, `fold-a-c`, `fold-b-d`, `fold-c-e`, `fold-d-f`, `fold-e-g`, `fold-f-h`, `fold-g-h`

(The actual graph depends on which letters are physical neighbors. Pick the bindings that match the unfolded geometry; the parser doesn't care about order.)

If there are explicit glue-tab panels, add `fold-<body-panel>-tab<letter>` for each.

### Anticipated `attach-points`

- `pivot-anchor` (shared with 65/66/67/69)
- A landing or two for the way the fork mates to 67 (the rear plate). Specifically: 067 carries `landing-c69`/`landing-d69`/`landing-e69`/`landing-f69` (receiving tabs from 069's anchor-bearing box). 068 likely has its own landings or tabs binding it to 069 too — but the legacy authoring on 067/069 doesn't suggest specific 68↔69 landings. **Confirm.**
- The pendulum rod (piece 70) passes *through* the prongs but isn't glued — that's a non-rigid mechanical relationship. It probably does NOT need an attach-point id; the prongs are just shaped to allow the rod through. (Compare: 072's bare `hole` for a hardware pin — different concept.)

### Anticipated `marks`

- Possibly closure landings if any of the body panels wrap to meet themselves (unlikely for a box-shaped fork; more likely all closures happen at glue tabs).
- No expected alignments unless the fork registers visually against another piece.

### Open questions for the canvas

1. **Glue tabs.** Does the fork have explicit glue-tab panels (like 069's `taba` through `tabf`), or do the body cells meet edge-to-edge with glue applied directly to the panel faces? Look for `+++`/`---` patterns along the perimeter. If glue tabs exist, name them `tab-<letter>` matching whatever letters are printed.
2. **Prong panels.** Are the two prongs each *single* panels (just two flat extensions), or do they have folded structure (e.g., U-channel cross-section to give them stiffness)? The embedded-labels description (`rectangular fold-up development`) suggests the body is a box, so prongs may also be folded into 3D shape — in which case they're each multiple panels.
3. **Connection to 069.** How does the fork attach to the anchor-bearing box (069)? 067 (rear plate) has explicit landings receiving 069's tabs. Does 068 mate via shared geometry with 069 (an analogous landings setup), or is 068 only attached via the shared pivot axle and rests against 069 without a glue surface?
4. **Letter→geometry mapping.** I'm guessing every cell letter is a distinct panel. **Verify** by counting closed regions on the unfolded development. If you find more or fewer than 8, the mapping isn't 1:1 and we'll need to adjust.
