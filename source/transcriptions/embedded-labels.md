# Embedded Labels and Notes

*All text and notation embedded directly on the cut-out plates and figure pages of* Make Your Own Working Paper Clock *by James Smith Rudolph (HarperCollins / Perennial 2003 reprint of Harper & Row 1983).*

This document captures the labels, instructions, and direction markings that are printed *on* the plates themselves — the kind of information that's separate from the prose instructions on pages 34–40. Piece numbers and tab letters (e.g., `a`, `b`, `c₂₅`, `b₉`) are not exhaustively reproduced — those are best read directly from `scans-clean/`. What's recorded here is everything else: handwritten-style instruction notes, axle assignments, direction arrows, figure captions.

---

## Notation conventions used on the plates

- **Solid line** — cut here.
- **Dashed line** (`- - - -`) — fold *away from yourself* (valley fold).
- **Plus-sign line** (`+ + + + +`) — fold *toward yourself* (mountain fold). Score from the blank side.
- **`+` mark** at a hole — axle pin-hole (no bearing).
- Letter-with-subscript labels like `b₄`, `c₂₅`, `a₂₉` — read as "the space marked b on this piece glues onto piece 4," etc.

## Panel A (pieces 1–2, 6, 7, 110)

Long horizontal frame strips. No prose-style notes; just piece numbers (1, 2, 6, 7, 110), interior cell letters (a, b, c, d, e, f, g, h, i with subscripts indicating connections to other pieces), and dashed/plus-line fold markings.

## Panel B (pieces 3, 5, 8, 12–16, 93, 112, 113–118)

Frame and bracket pieces (3, 5, 8 are vertical frame strips paired with 1, 2 from panel A). Two small "93" tabs labeled twice in small font. Pieces 12–16 are short horizontal bracket pieces. Pieces 113–116 are small brace-like pieces. Piece 112 is a long thin strip running down the left margin, paired with piece "112a" elsewhere (the double-strip motif).

## Panel C (pieces 9, 11, 20–25, 27, 28)

Mid-frame pieces. Long vertical strips (9, 11, 20) plus shorter brace pieces (12, 14, 16 — wait, those are on B; 23, 24, 25 are here as small flap-style pieces with internal sub-piece labels for connecting to pieces 22, etc.). Piece 25 has multiple small flap labels (`b₂₅, d₂₅, e₂₅, f₂₅, c₂₅, g₂₅`). Piece 27 has a small `28` at the bottom — pieces 27 and 28 are the small triangular pendulum-support spacers that pinch the pendulum blade in Fig. 2.

- **Piece 44** — small donut with cross-hatch (the pulley's small bearing disc).

**Direction marker — piece 100 (the pendulum bob):**
A round disc with two vertical arrows. Top label `F` with an upward arrow; bottom label `S` with a downward arrow. Per the prose instructions in §III, raising the bob speeds the clock and lowering it slows it. So `F` indicates the *Fast* direction (slide bob up) and `S` indicates the *Slow* direction (slide bob down).

- Piece 72 — pendulum blade (long thin strip with mounting-pin hole).
- Piece 92a — narrow tongue used in the hands assembly (also referenced in instructions).
- Piece 94 — pendulum-bob outer tube (the long extruded shape across the top of the spread).
- Pieces 95, 96 — pendulum hook (small folded shape, glued to 1mm cardboard).
- Piece 97 — large round disc, the pendulum-bob front face. Has the `Apply no glue here` rectangle.
- Piece 98 — short rectangular strip (the bowed retainer that holds piece 97 onto the rod).
- Piece 99 — large saw-toothed crescent (decorative trim around the bob — wraps the perimeter of 97).
- Piece 53 — small disc with starred center (the pinion-mount on the middle wheel).

The printed face of the clock. Rectangular tan/brown outer border, off-white inner field. ## Markings worth noting

- The `*` (asterisk/star) marks on panel I pieces 118, 119 and on the case pieces in Fig. 16 are *pin holes* — the case is held to the framework by pins through these stars.
- The `+` marks in axle holes and on hubs are *axle centers* — used to pre-pierce centers before assembly so the back face can be located accurately.
- Hatching (vertical lines, slashes) in cross-section drawings (especially Fig. 13) indicates a cut surface or a mounting bracket — it's a draftsman's convention here, not a labeled feature.
